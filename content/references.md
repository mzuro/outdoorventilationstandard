## Consolidated References

The following sources are cited across the Outdoor Ventilation Standard research program. This master bibliography consolidates all foundational references used in the 12 published research papers.

---

### Fire Plume Physics

- Heskestad, G. "Fire Plumes, Flame Height, and Air Entrainment." Chapter 13 in *SFPE Handbook of Fire Protection Engineering*, 5th ed. Springer, 2016.

- McCaffrey, B.J. "Purely Buoyant Diffusion Flames: Some Experimental Results." NBSIR 79-1910. National Bureau of Standards, 1979.

- Zukoski, E.E. "Properties of Fire Plumes." In *Combustion Fundamentals of Fire*, edited by G. Cox. Academic Press, 1995.

- Drysdale, D. *An Introduction to Fire Dynamics*, 3rd ed. John Wiley & Sons, 2011.

- Karlsson, B. and Quintiere, J.G. *Enclosure Fire Dynamics*. CRC Press, 2000.

---

### Turbulent Plume Theory

- Morton, B.R., Taylor, G.I., and Turner, J.S. "Turbulent Gravitational Convection from Maintained and Instantaneous Sources." *Proceedings of the Royal Society A*, 234 (1956): 1–23.

- Turner, J.S. *Buoyancy Effects in Fluids*. Cambridge University Press, 1973.

- List, E.J. "Turbulent Jets and Plumes." *Annual Review of Fluid Mechanics*, 14 (1982): 189–212.

---

### Atmospheric Dispersion and Wind Effects

- Briggs, G.A. "A Plume Rise Model Compared with Observations." *Journal of the Air Pollution Control Association*, 15, no. 9 (1965): 433–438.

- Briggs, G.A. "Plume Rise Predictions." In *Lectures on Air Pollution and Environmental Impact Analyses*, edited by D.A. Haugen. American Meteorological Society, 1975.

- Pasquill, F. and Smith, F.B. *Atmospheric Diffusion*, 3rd ed. Ellis Horwood, 1983.

---

### Ventilation Engineering

- ASHRAE. *ASHRAE Handbook — HVAC Applications*. Chapter 33: Kitchen Ventilation. American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers.

- ASHRAE Standard 154. *Ventilation for Commercial Cooking Operations*. American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers.

- ACGIH. *Industrial Ventilation: A Manual of Recommended Practice for Design*, 30th ed. American Conference of Governmental Industrial Hygienists.

---

### Building Codes and Standards

- International Code Council. *International Mechanical Code (IMC)*. Relevant sections on commercial kitchen exhaust.

- Underwriters Laboratories. *UL 710: Standard for Exhaust Hoods for Commercial Cooking Equipment*.

- Underwriters Laboratories. *UL 710B: Standard for Recirculating Systems*.

- National Fire Protection Association. *NFPA 96: Standard for Ventilation Control and Fire Protection of Commercial Cooking Operations*.

---

### Grease Aerosol and Particle Transport

- Schauer, J.J., Kleeman, M.J., Cass, G.R., and Simoneit, B.R.T. "Measurement of Emissions from Air Pollution Sources. 1. C1 through C29 Organic Compounds from Meat Charbroiling." *Environmental Science & Technology*, 33, no. 10 (1999): 1566–1577.

- Hildemann, L.M., Markowski, G.R., and Cass, G.R. "Chemical Composition of Emissions from Urban Sources of Fine Organic Aerosol." *Environmental Science & Technology*, 25, no. 4 (1991): 744–759.

- Hinds, W.C. *Aerosol Technology: Properties, Behavior, and Measurement of Airborne Particles*, 2nd ed. John Wiley & Sons, 1999.

---

### Thermal Radiation

- Incropera, F.P., DeWitt, D.P., Bergman, T.L., and Lavine, A.S. *Fundamentals of Heat and Mass Transfer*, 7th ed. John Wiley & Sons, 2011.

- Modest, M.F. *Radiative Heat Transfer*, 3rd ed. Academic Press, 2013.

---

### General Reference

- Cengel, Y.A. and Cimbala, J.M. *Fluid Mechanics: Fundamentals and Applications*, 4th ed. McGraw-Hill, 2018.

- Bejan, A. *Convection Heat Transfer*, 4th ed. John Wiley & Sons, 2013.
