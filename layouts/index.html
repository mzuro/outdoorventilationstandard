Why This Exists

Outdoor cooking ventilation operates under fundamentally different physical conditions than indoor kitchen ventilation, yet is routinely designed using indoor-derived assumptions. No existing standard — ASHRAE 154, the International Mechanical Code, or UL 710 — provides comprehensive, physics-based guidance for outdoor hood systems exposed to open boundaries, wind, and unconstrained plume dispersion.

This program addresses that gap through applied physics research grounded in established principles:

  • Fire science — Heskestad plume correlations
  • Fluid dynamics — Morton-Taylor-Turner entrainment theory
  • Atmospheric dispersion — Briggs plume rise equations
  • Ventilation engineering — ASHRAE & ACGIH analysis

No marketing language. No product recommendations. No ungrounded claims. Every conclusion is traceable to a stated physical mechanism with explicit assumptions and applicable ranges.
